
# CryptoViz Team Blogs

## Blog — 23F-0557

Working on CryptoViz was a highly valuable learning experience for me. 
Before this project, I understood AES encryption mostly through theoretical concepts taught in class. 
However, after implementing visual encryption demonstrations, I was finally able to understand how different encryption modes behave practically.

The most interesting part for me was the ECB visualization. 
Seeing image patterns remain visible after encryption clearly demonstrated why ECB mode is considered insecure. 
This project also improved my understanding of browser cryptography, SHA-256 hashing, and frontend architecture.

In the future, I would like to expand the project by adding more encryption algorithms and advanced cybersecurity visualizations.

## Blog — 23F-0624

One of my favorite parts of the project was implementing the AI randomness analyzer. 
The analyzer evaluates encryption quality using entropy, chi-square analysis, and byte correlation statistics.

Through this implementation, I learned how randomness is measured in cryptographic systems and why strong encryption should produce nearly uniform distributions.

The avalanche effect demonstration was another exciting feature because it visually showed how changing a single pixel causes massive encrypted output changes.

This project improved my understanding of cryptographic analysis and secure system design.
